Overview

Epoch 1: The Primordial Universe — You begin as a lone particle in an empty void. Through absorbing energy motes, converting energy to hydrogen via proton synthesis, and unlocking gravity, you progress through the formation of atoms, gas clouds, nebulae, stars, and eventually planets.

Mass is now fully derived from the atomic weights of elements you accumulate — it cannot be earned or spent directly.

The energy phase features an Aeon Prestige system: repeatedly reset your run to earn Aeons, which buy persistent meta upgrades that raise the energy cap from 500 toward 10,000,000. At 10M energy, an automatic Epoch Collapse (Big Crunch cinematic) awards an Epoch Echo and unlocks quark flavours — six coloured energy types with passive bonuses. A second Collapse unlocks the subatomic phase: quarks form protons, neutrons, and electrons.

The epoch ends when you reach the stable, Earth-like planet milestone.

Core Mechanics

Mote Absorption (Energy Generation)

Energy is primarily earned by absorbing mote particles.

Base energy per mote depends on quality tier:

QualityNameBase Value
0Common1
1Uncommon1.5
2Rare2.5
3Epic5
4Legendary10

Absorption multipliers stack multiplicatively:

finalEnergy = qualityBase × (quantumFluctuation^level) × (vacuumHarvesting^level)
            = qualityBase × 2.0^qfLevel × 1.8^vhLevel

Result is rounded up to a minimum of 1.

Clicking

Removed. Clicking on the canvas no longer generates energy. Energy is earned by the beacon mote drifting to the player (see Beacon Phase below) and by gravity absorption after Electromagnetic Bond is purchased.

Beacon Phase (pre-Electromagnetic Bond)

When the game starts, no ambient motes are spawned. Instead, a single glowing beacon mote (mote_rare sprite) drifts slowly toward the player from the right side of the screen.

The background scrolls at ~50 px/s (rightward + slight upward drift), implying the player is moving through space.

When the beacon mote reaches the player (contact radius 40 px), it is absorbed and awards 1 energy. This is just enough to purchase Electromagnetic Bond (cost: 1 energy).

On purchase:

  • The beacon mote is removed.
  • ~250 ambient void motes spawn (normal mote genesis begins).
  • Background scroll decelerates to a halt (~2 seconds).

Proton Synthesis (Energy → Hydrogen)

Replaces the old energy → mass conversion. Unlocked by purchasing Proton Forge.

A rate slider in the Fusion Lab panel (0–100%) controls how much energy per second flows into hydrogen production.

H/s = protonForgeRate × sliderFraction × energyAvailable
  • Energy is drained continuously; hydrogen accumulates
  • Default slider fraction: 0% (player must set manually)
  • Upgraded by Quantum Nucleogenesis (×1.5 H rate per level) and Energy Density (−15% energy cost per level)
  • Prestige bonus Primordial Rate adds +25% to the base rate

Mote Spawning

Motes spawn procedurally across the infinite universe. Two systems feed the supply:

  1. spawnParticle() — Random spawns with 60% weighted inside 2× gravity radius (sqrt distribution for uniform area coverage). 40% spawn randomly across the viewport.
  1. _spawnEdgeParticle() — Edge spawns with 50% chance between 0.8× and 2.5× gravity radius.

Spawn flash: Each new mote shows a tiny white flash (0.3s fade) at its spawn location.

Movement

Unlocked by Cosmic Drift (requires upg_gravitationalPull / Electromagnetic Bond).

Desktop: WASD or arrow keys to navigate. Mobile / touch: Touch-drag anywhere on the canvas to move the character toward the drag point. The character follows the touch position smoothly.

  • Ion Thrust: +20 speed per level (max 5 levels)
  • Manoeuvring Jets: +0.8 turn rate per level (max 3 levels)

Once movement is unlocked, the Space Dust parallax layer continues scrolling (it also scrolls during the beacon phase to imply drift).

Dark Matter Node System

Activated at ms_gasCloud. Nodes appear automatically when achievement thresholds are crossed — no upgrade gate required.

Dark matter is collected by navigating to nodes that spawn in the void. Each node must be physically reached.

Node creation — threshold-triggered:

ThresholdFirst BurstContinuous Rate
Energy ≥ 1,0001 node1 node per 5,000 energy
Energy ≥ 10,0002 nodes1 node per 2,500 energy
ms_firstAtom (H reached)5 nodes1 node per 5,000 H
ms_heliumAccumulated3 nodes1 node per 500 He
ms_carbonForged4 nodes1 node per 100 C
ms_firstWater2 nodes1 node per 100 H₂O
1st Prestige10 nodes+5 per prs_dmCapacity level

Each threshold is crossed only once per run (burst on first crossing), then continuous generation begins when the resource is at or above the rate threshold.

Node value: Flat 1 DM per node (compound factor removed). Prestige upgrades prs_darkFlow and prs_dmSiphon increase max nodes and collection radius.

Node lifecycle:

  1. Spawns at a random void position (margin ≥ 350 px from region edges).
  2. Flickers gently at very low opacity (barely visible in the dark void).
  3. Emits periodic gravity waves (interval 1.25–2.75 s per node) that push nearby energy motes outward.
  4. Collected when the player moves within the collection radius (default 60 px, +40 px from prs_dmSiphon).
  5. Removed from the field on collection; a darkMatter:collected event fires.

Gravity wave: Each pulse emits a circular force wave expanding at 360 px/s. The wave radius is dynamically sized to the current player distance + 200 px, ensuring every pulse just reaches the player. Nearby energy motes are pushed outward. The wave also visibly disrupts the space dust layer (dust displaced up to 18 px vertically at the wave front, snapping back immediately as the wave passes).

Pulse frequency: Interval of 1.25–2.75 s between pulses per node.

Reflected ripple: When the wave front reaches the player, a cosmic dust wave reflects back toward the dark matter node in a 60° cone (±30°, max 140 px, ~0.7 s duration). The cone is centred on the direction from the player toward the DM node, and dust within it is displaced — evoking a stone-in-pond reflection.

Space Dust Background

350 dust particles are seeded with a deterministic random seed and drawn at ultra-low opacity (0.014–0.056 alpha), providing a subtle spatial reference as the player moves.

  • Parallax factor: 0.20 (far layer) / 0.50 (near layer)
  • Beacon phase scroll: during the early game, dust scrolls at ~50 px/s (parallax-attenuated) along with the world scroll, implying movement through space. Scroll stops after Electromagnetic Bond is purchased.
  • Colour palette: 72% cool blue-white (#c8d8f8), 28% warm amber (#ffe8c0)
  • Tiling: normalised coordinates tile seamlessly across the viewport

Dark matter disruption: When a dark matter node is active, its vicinity is visually cleared:

  • A radial dark halo (radius ~55–95 px) darkens the immediate area around the node
  • Dust particles near the node are pushed outward (max 50 px displacement)
  • At the expanding wave front, dust is subtly brightened (no size expansion — prevents the ring-of-pixels artefact)

Star Lifecycle

Unlocked at ms_mainSequenceStar (hydrogen ≥ a defined threshold). Stars cycle through stages:

  1. Main Sequence → 2. Red Giant → 3. Supernova → 4. Neutron Star → 5. Second Generation

Each stage triggers milestones and unlocks upgrades. Elemental fusion runs continuously while a star is active (see Elemental Fusion System).

Elemental Fusion System

Driven by FusionEngine.js. H → He requires Fusion Ignition upgrade; He → C/O requires Red Giant Catalyst upgrade.

Main Sequence (H → He) — gated by upg_fusionIgnition

Rate: 1.0 He/s × hFusionMult × hToHeThrottle  (consumes 4H per 1He)

Red Giant (He → C and He → O) — gated by upg_redGiantCatalyst

Carbon rate:  0.3 C/s × heFusionMult × (1 - cOSplit)  (consumes 3He per 1C)
Oxygen rate:  0.3 O/s × heFusionMult × cOSplit         (consumes 4He per 1O)

Supernova (burst)

On stage entry: +10 × ironYieldMult  Fe (one-time burst)

Throttle and split controls are adjustable in the Fusion Lab panel (see below).

Hydrogen bootstrap — On ms_firstAtom reward:

hydrogen += mass × 0.1,  helium += mass × 0.025

**Fusion multiplier upgrades:**

| Upgrade | Effect Type | Magnitude/level |
|---------|-------------|-----------------|
| Hydrogen Fusion | `hFusionMult` | ×1.5 |
| Helium Ignition | `heFusionMult` | ×1.5 |
| Extended Main Sequence | `ironYieldMult` | ×1.5 |

Reactions are capped by ingredient availability. If hydrogen is scarce, helium production scales proportionally down.

### Physical Mass Display

The Resource Panel shows a sub-label under the **Mass** row with the physical mass in SI units, derived from the actual atomic weights of all accumulated elements.

**Formula:**

physicalMassGrams = Σ(element_amount × atomicWeight_amu) × 1.66054e-24 g/amu


**Atomic weights used:**

| Element | amu |
|---------|-----|
| Hydrogen | 1.008 |
| Helium | 4.003 |
| Carbon | 12.011 |
| Oxygen | 15.999 |
| Iron | 55.845 |

**SI scale (auto-ranging):**
ag → fg → pg → ng → µg → mg → g → kg → t → kt → Mt

Early game amounts land in the attogram–femtogram range (scientifically correct for atomic-scale matter).

## Prestige System (Aeon & Epoch Echo)

Two distinct prestige currencies serve two functions within the energy phase:

### Aeon Prestige (Meta, Player-Triggered)

- **When**: Player manually triggers when energy ≥ current energy cap.
- **Earns**: 1+ Aeons per run (base 1, +1 per Echo Chamber level).
- **Resets**: energy, upgrades, milestones, stars (in-run systems).
- **Persists**: Aeons total, all purchased Aeon upgrades, Epoch Echoes, quark allocations, subatomic state.

**Cap progression via Expanded Vacuum**: 500 → 5,000 → 50,000 → 500,000 → 5,000,000 → 10,000,000. Requires 4–5 Aeon prestiges to unlock the full 10M ceiling.

## Upgrades

### Cost Formula

For multi-level upgrades with exponential scaling:

cost(level) = baseCost × costScaling^(level - 1)


This pure exponential formula applies to all levels — costs grow steeply with each purchase.

For single-level upgrades: cost is simply `baseCost`.

Multi-element costs use a `costRecipe` array; all costs must be affordable simultaneously.

For **Quantum Capacitor**: cost equals the **current energy cap** at time of purchase. Each level raises the cap, so subsequent levels automatically cost more as the cap grows.

### Hard Scaling (L6+ Exponential Wall)

Some key upgrades have a `hardScalingStart` level, beyond which costs increase at an additional exponential rate:

cost(level) = normalCost(hardScalingStart) × hardScaling^(level - hardScalingStart)


| Upgrade | hardScalingStart | hardScaling |
|---------|-----------------|-------------|
| EM Bond | 5 | 5.0 |
| Quantum Fluctuation | 5 | 6.0 |
| Vacuum Harvesting | 5 | 4.0 |
| Mote Genesis | 5 | 4.5 |

L6–L10 are intentionally unreachable pre-Collapse. Quark bonuses (especially Bottom: -% upgrade costs) make them achievable post-Collapse.

### Effect Scaling Rules

| Effect Type | Scaling | Formula at Level N |
|-------------|---------|-------------------|
| `rateAdditive` | Linear | `magnitude × N` |
| `capIncrease` | Quadratic | `magnitude × N²` |
| `rateMultiplier` | Exponential | `magnitude^N` |
| `clickMultiplier` | Exponential | `magnitude^N` |
| `absorptionMultiplier` | Exponential | `magnitude^N` |
| `unlock` | Binary | Unlocks at level 1 |

**All multi-level upgrades are 10 levels** with `costScaling: 3×` and pure exponential cost growth across all levels.

**Live upgrade specs**: Each upgrade card shows the current cumulative effect (`Now: ×6.25`) and what the next level will give (`→ ×12.5`). For cost-reduction upgrades (Energy Density), shown as percentage (`Now: 56% cost/H`).

## Goal Widget

A persistent goal bar is displayed at the top of the Resource panel. It tracks the **next upcoming milestone** and shows live progress.

- **Title**: The milestone name (e.g. "Birth of Hydrogen")
- **Progress bar**: Fills proportionally from 0 → 100% as the tracked resource approaches its target
- **Label**: Shows `ResourceName: current / target` (formatted numbers)

For non-resource milestones (star cycle stages), the label shows the required condition; the bar shows 0% since progress cannot be quantified.

The widget auto-refreshes on every `resource:updated` event and immediately on `milestone:triggered`.

## Visual Thresholds

The player's home object evolves visually as mass increases:

- **Shape**: Always a **circle** (not a square/sprite).
- **Size**: Scales continuously with mass. The character's radius interpolates linearly between each visual threshold band as mass increases, growing smoothly rather than jumping at fixed milestones.
- Thresholds are defined in the epoch config's `visualThresholds` array and change the particle's appearance (colour, glow strength, base size). The rendering smoothly interpolates between threshold states.

When the home object crosses a visual threshold, a brief **white flash** occurs and a particle boost may trigger.

## Mobile UX

The game supports two distinct layouts:

### Responsive Breakpoint

| Viewport Width | Layout |
|----------------|--------|
| ≤ 600px | **Mobile** (bottom tab bar + drawer) |
| > 600px | **Desktop** (sidebar panels) |

This breakpoint correctly handles the **Samsung Galaxy Z Fold 6**:
- Phone mode (outer screen, ~323px CSS width) → mobile layout ✓
- Tablet mode (inner screen, ~673px CSS width) → desktop layout ✓

Layout switches live on resize (no page reload) via `window.matchMedia` change listener.

### Mobile Tab Bar

A fixed 56 px tab bar sits at the bottom of the screen on mobile. Five tabs:

| Tab | Panel |
|-----|-------|
| 📊 Stats | Stats panel |
| ⚡ Resources | Resource panel (with Goal widget) |
| 🔬 Upgrades | Upgrade panel |
| 📜 Chronicle | Chronicle / log panel |
| ✨ Legacy | Residual bonus panel |

Tapping a tab slides up a 50vh drawer from the bottom. Tapping again (or the ▼ button) collapses it.

When the drawer is open, the canvas **camera shifts upward** by ~40% of the drawer height so the player character remains centred in the visible viewport.

The tab bar uses `z-index: 200` with `transform: translateZ(0)` to guarantee it stays above the drawer during GPU compositing.

### Touch / Drag Movement

Once **Cosmic Drift** is purchased:
- **Mobile/touch**: Touch-drag on the canvas to steer. The character moves toward the drag point.
- **Desktop**: Same drag works with mouse click-and-hold.
- **Keyboard**: WASD / arrow keys (desktop only).

## Residual Bonus (Epoch Completion)

Upon completing Epoch 1 (reaching ms_stablePlanet), a **Cosmic Residue** bonus is calculated:

completionScore = (milestonesTriggered / 16) × 0.5 + (upgradesPurchased / totalUpgrades) × 0.5 bonusValue = 0.05 + completionScore × 0.15


> **Note**: `TOTAL_MILESTONES` and `TOTAL_UPGRADES` in code are legacy constants (16 and 23 respectively). The actual counts have grown to 21 milestones and ~47 upgrades. A future update will recalibrate these constants.

| Completion | Bonus |
|------------|-------|
| 0% | +5% all rates |
| 50% | +12.5% all rates |
| 100% | +20% all rates |

This bonus carries into future epochs as a permanent meta-progression boost.

## Formulas Reference

### Upgrade Cost at Level N

cost(N) = baseCost × costScaling^(N - 1)


### Absorption Energy Value

energy = ceil(qualityBase × 1.5^qfLevel × 1.3^vhLevel × 1.5^mrLevel × 2.0^maLevel)


Where:
- `qfLevel` = Quantum Fluctuation level (max 20)
- `vhLevel` = Vacuum Harvesting level (max 20)
- `mrLevel` = Mote Resonance level (max 15)
- `maLevel` = Mote Amplification level (max 12)

### Mass Conversion Rate

massPerSec = accretionLevel × sliderRate × 1.4^synthLevel × 1.5^forgeLevel energyDrainPerSec = 10 × accretionLevel × sliderRate pulseInterval = 2.0 × 0.8^rapidLevel × 0.7^singularityLevel (seconds)


### Gravity Effective Radius

effectiveRadius = (gravityRadiusByLevel[level] + tractorRange) × (1 + log₁₀(1 + mass) × 0.4)


### Gravity Pull Speed

t = max(0, 1 - distance / effectiveRadius) speed = (2 + 178 × t³) × tractorSpeedMult × massSpeedMult massSpeedMult = 1 + log₁₀(1 + mass) × 0.1


### Dark Matter Node Value

compoundFactor = 1.05 + (accelerantLevel × 0.03) + (lensingLevel × 0.05) nodeValue = compoundFactor ^ totalCollected spawnInterval = max(3, 20 − currentsLevel × 2) (seconds) collectRadius = 60 + siphonLevel × 40 (pixels) maxNodes = 1 + darkFlowLevel waveStrength = 180 + amplifier2Level × 80


### Mote Densification Mass

massPerMote = 0.1 × qualityMult × 1.5^densLevel


### Residual Bonus

bonus = 0.05 + ((triggered/16) × 0.5 + (purchased/totalUpgrades) × 0.5) × 0.15


### Quark Passive Bonus

bonus = allocationFraction × flavourStrength × log₁₀(energy + 1) × resonanceScale resonanceScale = 1.5 (if Flavour Resonance purchased), else 1.0


### Hard Scaling Cost (L6+)

cost(N) = normalCost(hardScalingStart) × hardScaling^(N - hardScalingStart) where N >= hardScalingStart normalCost(N) = baseCost × costScaling^(N - 1)


### Aeon Prestige Reward

aeonsEarned = 1 + echoChamberLevel


### Dynamic Energy Cap

effectiveCap = min(absoluteCap, dynamicCap + upgradeBonuses + persistentBonuses) dynamicCap = 500 × 10^(expandedVacuumLevel) absoluteCap = 10,000,000


### Subatomic Production

quarkRate = baseRate × (1 + upgradeMultiplier) proton: requires 2 quark_up + 1 quark_down neutron: requires 1 quark_up + 2 quark_down electronRate = baseRate × (1 + cascadeMultiplier) (requires Lepton Field)
